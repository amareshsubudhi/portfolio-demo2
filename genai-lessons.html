5 Lessons From Scaling Generative AI in the Enterprise

Sponsoring and co-architecting an enterprise-wide generative AI assistant taught me that the hardest problems in GenAI adoption are rarely about the model. Here's what actually determined whether the program succeeded.

Narrow Use Case Governance Retrieval Quality Change Management Business Impact

Lesson 1

Start With a Narrow, High-Value Use Case

Resist the urge to build a general-purpose platform on day one.

  • Pick one workflow with clear, measurable pain — not "AI for everything"
  • A single well-adopted use case builds the credibility to fund the next ten
  • Narrow scope makes evaluation, governance, and rollout tractable
  • Platform capabilities should emerge from repeated use cases, not precede them

Lesson 2

Governance and RBAC Are Part of the Architecture

Retrofitting access control and data governance after launch is far more expensive than designing it in.

  • Document- and field-level permissions must flow through to retrieval results
  • Every response should be auditable back to its source documents
  • PII and sensitive data handling need sign-off before pilot, not after
  • Legal, security, and compliance are design partners, not final-mile reviewers

Lesson 3

Retrieval Quality Determines Adoption

Users forgive an imperfect model far more readily than they forgive a wrong answer built on the wrong source.

  • Invest early in chunking strategy, metadata, and index freshness
  • Evaluate retrieval precision/recall separately from generation quality
  • Hybrid search (keyword + vector) consistently outperforms vector-only in practice
  • A better retriever usually beats a bigger model for enterprise knowledge tasks

Lesson 4

Change Management Wins or Loses the Rollout

The best assistant in the world fails if nobody trusts it or knows how to use it well.

  • Champion networks inside business units drive adoption faster than top-down mandates
  • Train people on how to prompt and verify, not just how to log in
  • Publish known limitations openly — it builds trust rather than eroding it
  • Close the feedback loop visibly: show users their input changed the product

Lesson 5

Measure Business Impact, Not Model Metrics

BLEU scores and latency dashboards don't win renewal conversations with the CFO.

  • Tie every use case to a business metric before it launches, not after
  • Track time saved, ticket deflection, and error reduction — not just usage counts
  • Report in the language of the business unit funding the work
  • Kill or rescope use cases that can't show impact within a quarter
